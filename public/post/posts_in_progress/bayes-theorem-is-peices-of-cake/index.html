Bayes Theorem as peices of cake

Jun 12, 2018 1 min read

Under construction - I’m not

Bayes theorem is that theorem on conditional probability.

\(P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A)*P(A)}{P(B)} = \frac{Union(A B)}{P(B)}\)

It is simple and elegant. Like a piece of cake. Or more precisely, like several peices of cake.
